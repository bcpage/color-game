River Crossing

Alcuin of York — Propositiones ad Acuendos Juvenes — c. 800 AD

A farmer must cross a river with a fox, a chicken, and a bag of grain. The boat holds the farmer and one other. Alone on a bank: the fox eats the chicken. The chicken eats the grain. The farmer is the only safe mediator.

Two solutions exist. Alcuin posed this problem around 800 AD in Propositiones ad Acuendos Juvenes (Problems to Sharpen the Young). It is one of the earliest recorded river-crossing puzzles.
